# Real Property Gains Tax (RPGT) — Malaysia (产业盈利税 / Cukai Keuntungan Harta Tanah)

RPGT is a tax on the gain (profit) from disposing of real property or shares in a Real Property Company (RPC). Verified against the LHDN RPGT rate schedule (current for YA2025).

## RPGT Rates by Disposer Category and Holding Period

The rate depends on WHO is disposing and HOW LONG the property was held (from the Sale & Purchase Agreement date to the disposal date).

### Malaysian Citizens and Permanent Residents (individuals)
- Disposal within 3 years (1st–3rd year): **30%**
- Disposal in the 4th year: **20%**
- Disposal in the 5th year: **15%**
- Disposal in the 6th year and onwards: **0%** (no RPGT)

Note: the 6th-year-onwards 0% rate for citizens/PR has applied since 1 January 2022 (Budget 2022). The older 5% rate no longer applies to citizens/PR.

### Companies (incorporated in Malaysia)
- Within 3 years: **30%**
- 4th year: **20%**
- 5th year: **15%**
- 6th year and onwards: **10%**

### Non-citizens / Foreigners / Foreign companies
- Within 5 years: **30%**
- 6th year and onwards: **10%**

## How RPGT is Calculated

Chargeable gain = Disposal price − Acquisition price − Allowable expenses
(allowable expenses include legal fees, agent/brokerage commission, renovation/enhancement costs, advertising to find a buyer).

For individuals, a **Schedule 4 exemption** applies: the GREATER of RM10,000 or 10% of the chargeable gain is exempt.

Net chargeable gain = Chargeable gain − exemption.
RPGT payable = Net chargeable gain × applicable rate.

### Worked example
A Malaysian citizen sells a property in the 5th year of holding with a chargeable gain of RM200,000:
- Schedule 4 exemption = greater of RM10,000 or 10% × RM200,000 = RM20,000
- Net chargeable gain = RM200,000 − RM20,000 = RM180,000
- 5th year rate for citizens = 15%
- RPGT payable = RM180,000 × 15% = **RM27,000**

If the same citizen sold in the 6th year onwards: rate is 0%, so RPGT = RM0.

## Key Exemptions

- **Once-in-a-lifetime exemption**: an individual (citizen/PR) is exempt on the gain from disposing of ONE private residence (house, condo, apartment) in their lifetime.
- **Low/medium-cost housing**: disposal of low/medium-cost homes valued below RM200,000 is exempt for citizens.
- **Transfers between family members** (love and affection: husband-wife, parent-child, grandparent-grandchild) may be exempt / deemed no gain no loss.

## Filing (Self-Assessment from 2025)

From 1 January 2025, RPGT moved to a self-assessment system. The disposer must compute the RPGT and file electronically (e-CKHT) via the MyTax portal within 60 days of the disposal date. Both the disposer and acquirer have filing obligations; the acquirer's solicitor typically retains a portion of the price pending RPGT clearance.
