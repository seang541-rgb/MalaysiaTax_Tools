## CP204 - Estimated Tax Payable (预估税申报)

### Who Must Submit
- ALL companies, LLPs, trust bodies and co-operatives must submit Form CP204
  (estimate of tax payable) no later than 30 days BEFORE the beginning of the
  basis period (financial year).
- New companies: submit within 3 months of commencing operations
  (if first basis period is not less than 6 months).

### Minimum Estimate Rule
- The estimate cannot be less than 85% of the revised estimate (or original
  estimate if no revision) for the immediately preceding year of assessment.

### Monthly Instalments (CP207)
- The estimated tax is paid in equal monthly instalments starting from the
  2nd month of the basis period.
- Payment due by the 15th of each month.
- Late instalment: 10% penalty on the unpaid amount.

### Revisions (CP204A)
- Companies may revise the estimate in the 6th and/or 9th month of the basis
  period (and 11th month for YA2024-2026 under a temporary concession).

### Underestimation Penalty
- If actual tax exceeds the (revised) estimate by MORE than 30%, the difference
  above the 30% buffer is subject to a 10% penalty (Section 107C(10)).
- Example: estimate RM100k, actual tax RM200k. 30% buffer = RM60k.
  Excess = RM200k - RM100k - RM60k = RM40k. Penalty = RM4,000.

### SME Exemption
- New SMEs (paid-up capital ≤ RM2.5M, revenue < RM50M) are NOT required to
  submit CP204 for the first 2 years of assessment from commencement.

## Capital Allowance (资本减免)

Accounting depreciation is NOT tax deductible. Instead, businesses claim
capital allowances on qualifying plant & machinery.

### Standard Rates
| Asset Type | Initial Allowance | Annual Allowance |
|---|---|---|
| Heavy machinery, motor vehicles | 20% | 20% |
| Plant & machinery (general) | 20% | 14% |
| Computers & ICT equipment | 20% | 20% |
| Office equipment, furniture | 20% | 10% |
| Industrial buildings | 10% | 3% |

### Special Rules
- Small value assets (≤ RM2,000 each): 100% write-off, capped at RM20,000
  total per YA for non-SMEs (SMEs: no cap).
- Motor vehicles (non-commercial): qualifying expenditure capped at RM100,000
  (new vehicle ≤ RM150,000) or RM50,000 (others).
- Unabsorbed capital allowances can be carried forward indefinitely (but only
  for the same business source).

## Deductible vs Non-Deductible Expenses (可扣除费用)

### General Rule (Section 33(1))
Expenses must be wholly and exclusively incurred in the production of gross
income to be deductible.

### Common NON-deductible items
- Accounting depreciation (claim capital allowance instead)
- Private/domestic expenses
- Fines and penalties (traffic fines, tax penalties)
- Entertainment: generally only 50% deductible; 100% only in specific cases
  (e.g. entertainment for employees, entertainment that is the business itself)
- Pre-commencement expenses (with limited exceptions)
- Withdrawal of capital, provisions/unrealised amounts
- Lease rental of non-commercial motor vehicle above the cap
- Expenses without a valid e-invoice (increasingly enforced from 2026)

### Common deductible items (sometimes missed)
- Employer EPF contributions (up to 19% of employee remuneration)
- Approved donations (capped at 10% of aggregate income)
- Secretarial fee + tax filing fee (combined cap RM15,000 per YA)
- Renovation expenses under specific schemes (when gazetted)

## Business Losses (亏损结转)

- Current-year business losses can be set off against ALL income sources in
  the same year (business and non-business).
- Unabsorbed losses are carried forward and set off ONLY against business income.
- TIME LIMIT: losses can be carried forward for a MAXIMUM of 10 consecutive
  years of assessment (rule effective from YA2019; pre-2019 losses were deemed
  to start from YA2019).
- Losses cannot be carried forward if there is a substantial change (>50%) in
  shareholders for dormant companies.
- No loss carry-back in Malaysia (except special COVID-era concessions, now lapsed).

## Withholding Tax (WHT 预扣税)

Payments by Malaysian residents to NON-RESIDENTS are subject to withholding tax:

| Payment Type | Section | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Contract payments (services under contract) | 107A | 10% + 3% |
| Interest | 109 | 15% |
| Royalties | 109 | 10% |
| Technical/management service fees (performed in Malaysia) | 109B | 10% |
| Rental of movable property | 109B | 10% |
| Public entertainers | 109A | 15% |
| Other gains under s4(f) | 109F | 10% |

- WHT must be remitted to LHDN within 30 days of paying/crediting.
- Failure: payment becomes non-deductible + 10% penalty on unpaid WHT.
- Double Tax Agreement (DTA) rates may reduce these — check the specific treaty.

## Capital Gains Tax (CGT) — from 2024

- Disposal of UNLISTED Malaysian company shares by companies/LLPs/trusts:
  10% on net gain (or 2% on gross consideration for pre-2024 acquisitions).
- Effective 1 January 2026, the definitions of "shares" and "disposal" were
  amended to confirm CGT also applies to winding-up/dissolution distributions
  and redemption/conversion of preference shares.
- Foreign capital asset gains received in Malaysia by residents: exempt if
  economic substance requirements met (exemption extended to 31 December 2030).
- Individuals are NOT subject to CGT on unlisted shares (only companies and
  similar entities).

## Dividend Tax (股息税) — from YA2025

- Malaysia operates the single-tier system: corporate profits are taxed once;
  dividends are generally tax-free for shareholders.
- NEW from YA2025: INDIVIDUAL shareholders pay 2% tax on annual dividend income
  exceeding RM100,000 (the excess above RM100k is taxed).
- From 1 January 2026, distributions from LLPs to individual partners above
  RM100,000 are also subject to the 2% tax.
- Companies receiving dividends remain exempt (single-tier).
- Foreign-sourced dividends received by resident companies/LLPs: exempt
  (subject to conditions), extended to 31 December 2030.

## Filing Deadlines (Form C)
- Form C (corporate return): within 7 months after the end of the financial year
  (8 months for e-filing in practice via grace period).
- Audited accounts: required for most Sdn Bhd (phased audit exemption available
  for qualifying small companies from FY2025).
