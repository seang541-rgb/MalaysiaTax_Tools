Source: Royal Malaysian Customs Department (RMCD/JKDM), Ministry of Finance

## Overview
SST replaced GST in Malaysia from 1 September 2018. It consists of two separate taxes:
1. Sales Tax — on manufactured/imported goods
2. Service Tax — on prescribed taxable services

**Major update: From 1 July 2025, the service tax scope was significantly expanded**
to cover rental/leasing, construction, financial services, private healthcare and
private education. A penalty-free grace period ran 1 July – 31 December 2025;
full enforcement began 1 January 2026.

## Sales Tax

### Rates
| Category | Rate |
|---|---|
| General manufactured goods | 10% |
| Specific goods (certain food items, building materials) | 5% |
| Essential goods (basic food, medicines) | 0% / exempt |

From 1 July 2025 the sales tax rates were also revised on a targeted basis:
essential goods remain 0%, while selected non-essential/premium imported goods
moved to 5% or 10%.

### Who Must Register
- Manufacturers of taxable goods with annual sales value exceeding RM500,000
- Importers are taxed at point of import

## Service Tax (effective 1 July 2025)

### Rates and Registration Thresholds by Category
| Category | Rate | Registration Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| General services (professional, IT, consulting, hotels, insurance) | 8% | RM500,000 |
| Food & beverage (restaurants, catering) | 6% | RM1,500,000 |
| Telecommunications | 6% | RM500,000 |
| Vehicle parking | 6% | RM500,000 |
| Logistics | 6% | RM500,000 |
| **Rental / leasing (non-residential) — NEW Jul 2025** | 8% | RM1,000,000 |
| **Construction (non-residential) — NEW Jul 2025** | 6% | RM1,500,000 |
| **Financial services (fee/commission-based) — NEW Jul 2025** | 8% | RM1,000,000 |
| **Private healthcare for non-citizens — NEW Jul 2025** | 6% | RM1,500,000 |
| **Private education — NEW Jul 2025** | 6% | No threshold |
| Credit/charge card services | RM25 per card per year | No threshold |

### Important Notes on New Categories (July 2025)
- **Rental/leasing**: residential property rental is NOT taxable. Exemptions also
  cover reading materials, assets located outside Malaysia, financial leases, and
  rentals within Special/Designated Areas. MSME tenants with annual sales below
  RM1 million can be exempted from paying the tax on rental.
- **Construction**: residential buildings (and related public facilities) are NOT
  taxable. Rate is 6% for qualifying construction work.
- **Financial services**: only fee/commission-based services are taxed (8%).
  Interest, financing margin, and Islamic profit-rate equivalents are NOT taxed.
- **Private healthcare**: 6% applies only to services provided to NON-CITIZENS.
  Services to Malaysian citizens remain not taxable.
- **Private education**: 6% applies to private/international schools charging more
  than RM60,000 per student per academic year, and to higher education provided
  to non-citizen students.
- **Beauty services** (manicure, facial, hairdressing) were originally proposed for
  taxation but the proposal was WITHDRAWN before 1 July 2025 — they remain NOT taxable.

### Registration
- Must register within prescribed period after exceeding the threshold
- Apply via MySST system (mysst.customs.gov.my)
- Grace period: no prosecution/penalties until 31 December 2025 for businesses
  taking steps to comply; full enforcement from 1 January 2026

## Key Compliance

### Filing & Payment
- Taxable period: Every 2 months (bi-monthly)
- Return (SST-02) due: Within 30 days after end of taxable period
- Payment: Together with return submission

### Penalties
- Late registration: fine up to RM30,000 or 2 years imprisonment or both
- Late payment: penalty up to 40% in stages (10% + 15% + 15%)
- Failure to file: fine up to RM50,000 or 3 years imprisonment or both

## Input Tax Credit
- Unlike GST, SST has NO input tax credit mechanism
- Sales tax paid on inputs cannot be offset against output tax
- Business-to-business (B2B) exemptions exist for certain same-industry services
  to reduce cascading (e.g. group relief, B2B professional services exemption)
