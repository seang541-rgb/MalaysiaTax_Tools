# SST (Sales & Service Tax) - Malaysia 2025
Source: Royal Malaysian Customs Department (RMCD/JKDM)

## Overview
SST replaced GST in Malaysia from 1 September 2018. It consists of two separate taxes:
1. Sales Tax — on manufactured/imported goods
2. Service Tax — on prescribed taxable services

## Sales Tax

### Rates
| Category | Rate |
|---|---|
| General manufactured goods | 10% |
| Specific goods (petroleum, certain food items) | 5% |
| Exempted goods | 0% |

### Who Must Register
- Manufacturers of taxable goods with annual sales value exceeding RM500,000
- Importers are taxed at point of import

### Exemptions
- Exported goods
- Goods manufactured for export
- Certain essential items (basic food, medical equipment)

## Service Tax

### Rate
| All prescribed services | 8% (effective March 2024, increased from 6%) |

### Prescribed Taxable Services
1. Hotels and accommodation
2. Food & beverage (restaurants, bars, catering)
3. Telecommunications and internet services
4. Insurance and takaful
5. Professional services (legal, accounting, consulting, architecture, engineering)
6. Parking services
7. Electricity supply
8. Logistics services
9. Brokerage and underwriting
10. Credit/charge card services
11. Cleaning and security services
12. Domestic flights

### Registration Threshold
- Annual value of taxable services exceeding RM500,000
- Must register within 28 days of exceeding threshold
- Voluntary registration also available

### Exemptions from Service Tax
- Exported services (B2B cross-border)
- Government services
- Certain financial services

## Key Compliance

### Registration
- Apply via MySST system (mysst.customs.gov.my)
- Must display SST registration number

### Filing & Payment
- Taxable period: Every 2 months (bi-monthly)
- Return due: Within 30 days after end of taxable period
- Payment: Together with return submission

### Penalties
- Late registration: RM30,000 fine or 2 years imprisonment or both
- Late payment: 10% penalty on unpaid amount
- Failure to file: RM50,000 fine or 3 years imprisonment or both

## Input Tax Credit
- Unlike GST, SST has NO input tax credit mechanism
- Sales tax paid on raw materials cannot be offset against sales tax on finished goods
- This is a key difference from the former GST system
