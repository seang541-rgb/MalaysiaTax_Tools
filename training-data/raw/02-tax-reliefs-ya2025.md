# Malaysia Individual Tax Reliefs - YA2025

# Pelepasan Cukai Individu Malaysia - YA2025
Source: LHDN / RinggitPlus compilation

## Personal Reliefs

### 1. Individual & Dependent Relatives
- Amount: RM9,000 (automatic for all resident taxpayers)

### 2. Disabled Individual
- Amount: RM7,000 (additional)
- Condition: Certified by Department of Social Welfare (JKM)

### 3. Spouse Relief
- Amount: Up to RM4,000
- Condition: Spouse has no income or elects for joint assessment

### 4. Disabled Spouse
- Amount: RM6,000 (additional)
- Condition: Spouse certified disabled by JKM

### 5. Child (Under 18)
- Amount: RM2,000 per unmarried child

### 6. Child (18+ in full-time education)
- Amount: RM8,000 (diploma/degree in Malaysia or overseas)
- Amount: RM2,000 (other qualifying education)

### 7. Disabled Unmarried Child
- Amount: RM8,000

### 8. Disabled Child in Higher Education
- Amount: Additional RM8,000

## Medical & Health

### 9. Medical Expenses (Self/Spouse/Child)
- Amount: Up to RM10,000
- Covers: Serious diseases, fertility treatment, vaccinations (RM1,000 sub-limit), dental (RM1,000 sub-limit), medical check-ups (RM1,000 sub-limit), mental health
- Child disabilities sub-limit: RM6,000

### 10. Parents/Grandparents Medical Care
- Amount: Up to RM8,000
- Covers: Medical, dental, special needs, nursing care
- Check-ups/vaccinations sub-limit: RM1,000

### 11. Disabled Supporting Equipment
- Amount: Up to RM6,000
- Items: Wheelchairs, hearing aids, dialysis machines, prosthetics

## Education

### 12. Education Fees (Self)
- Amount: Up to RM7,000
- Covers: Postgraduate, vocational courses at recognized institutions
- Sub-limit: RM2,000 for skills enhancement/upskilling

### 13. SSPN (National Education Savings)
- Amount: Up to RM8,000
- Basis: Net deposits (deposits minus withdrawals in same year)

## Lifestyle

### 14. Lifestyle Relief
- Amount: Up to RM2,500
- Items: Books, journals, smartphones, tablets, computers, internet subscriptions, skills courses

### 15. Sports Lifestyle Relief
- Amount: Up to RM1,000 (separate from general lifestyle)
- Items: Sports equipment, facility fees, competition registration, gym memberships

### 16. EV Charging / Composting Equipment
- Amount: Up to RM2,500
- Items: Electric vehicle charging equipment or food waste composting machines

## Insurance & Contributions

### 17. Life Insurance & EPF
- Amount: Up to RM7,000 combined
- EPF employee contributions + life insurance/takaful premiums

### 18. Private Retirement Scheme (PRS)
- Amount: Up to RM3,000
- Extended through YA2030

### 19. Education/Medical Insurance
- Amount: Up to RM4,000
- Covers: Premiums for self, spouse, or children

### 20. SOCSO & EIS Contributions
- Amount: Up to RM350

## Housing

### 21. Housing Loan Interest (First Home)
- Amount: RM7,000 (property ≤ RM500,000) or RM5,000 (RM500,001-RM750,000)
- Duration: 3 consecutive years
- Condition: SPA signed between 1 Jan 2025 - 31 Dec 2027

## Family

### 22. Childcare / Kindergarten Fees
- Amount: Up to RM3,000
- Condition: Registered facilities, children aged 6 and below

### 23. Breastfeeding Equipment
- Amount: Up to RM1,000
- Frequency: Claimable once every 2 years
- Condition: Children aged 2 and below

## Tax Deductions (separate from reliefs)

### Donations
- Limit: 10% of aggregate income
- Must be to approved institutions with official receipts

### Professional Memberships
- No fixed maximum
- Must relate directly to profession

## Tax Rebates

### Personal Rebate
- Amount: RM400 if chargeable income ≤ RM35,000

### Spouse Rebate
- Amount: Additional RM400 if spouse has no income

### Zakat & Fitrah
- Amount: Full amount paid, deducted directly from tax

### Departure Levy (Religious Travel)
- Amount: Levy paid for umrah/religious pilgrimage
