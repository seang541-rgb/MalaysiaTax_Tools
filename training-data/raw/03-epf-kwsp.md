# EPF (KWSP) Contribution Rates - 2025
Source: kwsp.gov.my

## Employer Contribution Rates

### Malaysian / Permanent Resident Employees
| Employee Age | Monthly Wages ≤ RM5,000 | Monthly Wages > RM5,000 |
|---|---|---|
| Below 60 | 13% | 12% |
| 60 - 65 | 4% | 4% |
| Above 65 | 0% (exempt) | 0% (exempt) |

### Employee Contribution Rates
| Employee Age | Rate |
|---|---|
| Below 60 | 11% |
| 60 - 65 | 0% (voluntary) |
| Above 65 | 0% (voluntary) |

## Wage Ceiling
- Maximum insurable wages: RM20,000/month
- For wages exceeding RM20,000, contributions calculated on RM20,000

## Non-Malaysian Citizen Employees (effective October 2025)
- Employer: 2% of monthly wages
- Employee: 2% of monthly wages
- Both are mandatory

## Calculation Rules
1. Employers MUST use the Third Schedule table (not exact percentage) for wages ≤ RM20,000
2. For wages > RM20,000, calculate using exact percentage
3. Total contribution with cents shall be rounded to the next ringgit
4. Contributions must be paid by the 15th of the following month

## EPF Account Structure
- Account 1 (Akaun Persaraan): 75% of contributions — for retirement
- Account 2 (Akaun Sejahtera): 25% of contributions — withdrawable for housing, education, medical
- Account 3 (Akaun Fleksibel): Voluntary flexible savings

## Tax Relief for EPF
- Employee EPF contributions: Relief up to RM4,000 (combined with life insurance under RM7,000 cap)
- Employer EPF contributions: Tax deductible as business expense

## Penalties for Late Payment
- Employer who fails to pay on time: RM10,000 fine or imprisonment up to 3 years or both
- Plus dividend loss compensation
