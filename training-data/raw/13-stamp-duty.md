# Stamp Duty — Malaysia (印花税 / Duti Setem)

Stamp duty is charged on instruments (documents), most commonly on property transfers and loan agreements. Verified against PwC Malaysia / LHDN (current for 2025).

## Instrument of Transfer (MOT) — Ad Valorem Rates

Stamp duty on the transfer of property is progressive, charged on the higher of the purchase price or market value:

- First RM100,000: **1%**
- RM100,001 to RM500,000: **2%**
- RM500,001 to RM1,000,000: **3%**
- Above RM1,000,000: **4%**

### Worked example — RM500,000 property
- 1% × RM100,000 = RM1,000
- 2% × RM400,000 (the portion from RM100,001 to RM500,000) = RM8,000
- Total MOT stamp duty = **RM9,000**

### Worked example — RM1,500,000 property
- 1% × RM100,000 = RM1,000
- 2% × RM400,000 = RM8,000
- 3% × RM500,000 = RM15,000
- 4% × RM500,000 = RM20,000
- Total = **RM44,000**

## Loan Agreement Stamp Duty

Stamp duty on a loan/financing agreement is a flat **0.5%** of the loan amount.
Example: a RM450,000 loan → 0.5% × RM450,000 = RM2,250.

## Foreign Buyers

Non-citizen individuals and foreign-owned companies (excluding permanent residents) currently pay a flat **4%** MOT stamp duty on residential property, regardless of price. (Budget 2026 proposed raising this to a flat rate of up to 8% for instruments executed on/after 1 January 2026 — verify current status before relying on it.)

## First-Time Homebuyer Exemption

First-time buyers of residential property priced up to **RM500,000** receive a **100% exemption** on BOTH the instrument of transfer (MOT) and the loan agreement stamp duty. This incentive has been extended to 31 December 2027.

So a Malaysian first-time buyer purchasing a RM450,000 home pays **RM0** stamp duty (both MOT and loan exempt).

## Stamping Deadline & Penalties

Instruments should be stamped within 30 days of execution. Late stamping penalties escalate with delay (e.g. RM50 or 10% of the deficient duty, whichever is higher, if within 3 months; higher beyond that).
